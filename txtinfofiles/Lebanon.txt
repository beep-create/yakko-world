Lebanon, officially known as the Lebanese Republic, is a country in Western Asia. <br>
It is bordered by Syria to the north and east and Israel to the south, while Cyprus lies just west of it across the Mediterranean Sea. <br>
Lebanon's location at the crossroads of the Mediterranean Basin and the Arabian hinterland has contributed to its rich history <br>
and shaped a cultural identity of religious diversity.<br>
 Lebanon is home to roughly 6 million people and covers a territory of just 10,452 km2 (4,036 mi2), <br>
 making it among the smallest countries in Asia by land area. <br>
 Arabs comprise the overwhelming majority of the country's population, while Arabic is the official language, <br>
 and spoken Lebanese Arabic is used alongside Modern Standard Arabic in daily life. <br>

Denmark, officially the Kingdom of Denmark, is a Nordic country in Northern Europe. <br>
Denmark, which is the southernmost of the Scandinavian countries, consists of a peninsula, Jutland, <br>
and an archipelago of 443 named islands, with the largest being Zealand, Funen and the North Jutlandic Island. <br>
The islands are characterised by flat, arable land and sandy coasts, low elevation and a temperate climate. <br>
Denmark lies southwest of Sweden and south of Norway, and is bordered to the south by Germany. <br>
The Kingdom of Denmark is constitutionally a unitary state comprising Denmark proper and the two autonomous territories in the North Atlantic Ocean: <br>
the Faroe Islands and Greenland.<br>
 Denmark has a total area of 42,943 km2 (16,580 sq mi) as of 2020, and the total area including Greenland and the Faroe Islands is 2,210,579 km2 (853,509 sq mi).<br>
  Denmark proper has a population of 5.84 million (as of 2021), of which 800,000 live in the capital and largest city Copenhagen.<br>

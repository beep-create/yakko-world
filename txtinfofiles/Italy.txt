Italy, officially the Italian Republic, is a country consisting of a peninsula delimited by the Alps and several islands surrounding it.<br>
 Italy is located in south-central Europe,[16][17] and is also considered part of Western Europe.<br>
  A unitary parliamentary republic with Rome as its capital and largest city, the country covers a total area of 301,340 km2 (116,350 sq mi) <br>
  and shares land borders with France, Switzerland, Austria, Slovenia, and the enclaved microstates of Vatican City and San Marino. <br>
  Italy has a territorial enclave in Switzerland (Campione) and a maritime exclave in Tunisian waters (Lampedusa). <br>
  With around 60 million inhabitants, Italy is the third-most populous member state of the European Union. <br>

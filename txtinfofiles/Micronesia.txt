Micronesia is a subregion of Oceania, consisting of thousands of small islands in the western Pacific Ocean. <br>
It has a close shared cultural history with three other island regions: the Philippines to the west, Polynesia to the east, <br>
and Melanesia to the south—as well as with the wider community of Austronesian peoples.<br>
The region has a tropical marine climate and is part of the Oceanian realm. <br>
It includes four main archipelagos—the Caroline Islands, the Gilbert Islands, the Mariana Islands, and the Marshall Islands <br>
as well as numerous islands that are not part of any archipelago.<br>

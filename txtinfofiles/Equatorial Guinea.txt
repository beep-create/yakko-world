Equatorial Guinea, officially the Republic of Equatorial Guinea,  is a country on the west coast of Central Africa, <br>
with an area of 28,000 square kilometres (11,000 sq mi). <br>
Formerly the colony of Spanish Guinea, its post-independence name evokes its location near both the Equator and the Gulf of Guinea. <br>
As of 2015, the country had a population of 1,225,367. <br>
 It is the location of Bata, Equatorial Guinea's largest city, and Ciudad de la Paz, the country's planned future capital. <br>
 Rio Muni also includes several small offshore islands, such as Corisco, Elobey Grande, and Elobey Chico. <br>
 The country is a member of the African Union, Francophonie, OPEC and the CPLP. <br>

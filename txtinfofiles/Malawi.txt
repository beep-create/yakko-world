Malawi, officially the Republic of Malawi, is a landlocked country in southeastern Africa that was formerly known as Nyasaland.<br>
 It is bordered by Zambia to the west, Tanzania to the north and northeast, and Mozambique to the east, south and southwest. <br>
 Malawi spans over 118,484 km2 (45,747 sq mi) and has an estimated population of 19,431,566 (as of January 2021).<br>
Malawi's capital (and largest city) is Lilongwe. <br>
   Its second-largest is Blantyre, its third-largest is Mzuzu and its fourth-largest is its former capital, Zomba.<br>
    The name Malawi comes from the Maravi, an old name for the Chewa people who inhabit the area. <br>
    The country is nicknamed "The Warm Heart of Africa" because of the friendliness of its people.<br>

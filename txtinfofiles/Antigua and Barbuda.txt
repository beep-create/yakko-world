Antigua and Barbuda  is a sovereign island country in the West Indies in the Americas, lying between the Caribbean Sea and the Atlantic Ocean. It consists of two major islands, <br>
Antigua and Barbuda separated by 63 km (39 mi), and smaller islands. <br>
 The permanent population number is about 97,120 (2019 est.), with 97% being resident on Antigua. <br> 
  The capital and largest port and city is St. John's on Antigua, with Codrington being the largest town on Barbuda. <br>

   Following self-governance in its internal affairs, independence was granted from the United Kingdom on 1 November 1981. <br>

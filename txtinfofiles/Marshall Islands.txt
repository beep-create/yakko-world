The Marshall Islands, officially the Republic of the Marshall Islands, is an independent island country near the Equator in the Pacific Ocean, <br>
slightly west of the International Date Line. <br>
Geographically, the country is part of the larger island group of Micronesia. <br>
The country's population of 58,413 people is spread out over 5 islands and 29 coral atolls, comprising 1,156 individual islands and islets. <br>
The capital and largest city is Majuro. <br>
It has the largest portion of its territory made of water of any sovereign state, at 97.87%.<br>
The islands share maritime boundaries with Wake Island to the north, Kiribati to the southeast, Nauru to the south, and Federated States of Micronesia to the west.<br>
 About 52.3% of Marshall Islanders (27,797 at the 2011 Census) live on Majuro.<br>
  Data from the United Nations indicates an estimated population in 2018 of 58,413.  <br>
  In 2016, 73.3% of the population were defined as being "urban". <br>
  The UN also indicates a population density of 295 per km2 (765 people per mi2) and its projected 2020 population is 59,190.<br>

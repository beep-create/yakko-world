Algeria, officially the People's Democratic Republic of Algeria, is a country in the Maghreb region of North Africa. <br>
It is the largest country by total area in Africa, and by extension, the Arab world, and is bordered to the northeast by Tunisia; <br>
to the east by Libya; to the southeast by Niger; to the southwest by Mali, Mauritania, and Western Sahara; <br>
to the west by Morocco; and to the north by the Mediterranean Sea. <br>
The country has a semi-arid geography, with most of the population living in the fertile north and the Sahara dominating the geography of the south. <br>
Algeria covers an area of 2,381,741 square kilometres (919,595 sq mi), with a population of 44 million, and is the ninth-most populous country in Africa. <br>
The capital and largest city is Algiers, located in the far north, on the Mediterranean coast. <br>
The currency is the Algerian dinar. <br>

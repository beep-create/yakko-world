Greenland, is the world's largest island, located between the Arctic and Atlantic oceans, east of the Canadian Arctic Archipelago.<br>
 Greenland is an autonomous territory within the Kingdom of Denmark. <br>
 Though physiographically a part of the continent of North America, <br>
 Greenland has been politically and culturally associated with Europe for more than a millennium, beginning in 986.<br>
 The majority of its residents are Inuit, whose ancestors migrated from Alaska through Northern Canada, <br>
 gradually settling across the island by the 13th century.<br>
 With a population of 56,081 (2020), it is the least densely populated region in the world. <br>

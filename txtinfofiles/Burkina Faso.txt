Burkina Faso is a landlocked country in West Africa that covers an area of around 274,200 square kilometres (105,900 sq mi). <br>
 Burkina Faso is bordered by Mali to the northwest, Niger to the northeast, Benin to the southeast, <br>
 Togo and Ghana to the south, and the Ivory Coast to the southwest. <br>
 The July 2019 population estimate by the United Nations was 20,321,378. <br>
  Previously called Republic of Upper Volta (1958–1984), it was renamed "Burkina Faso" on 4 August 1984 by President Thomas Sankara. <br>
  Its citizens are known as Burkinabé or Burkinabè, and its capital and largest city is Ouagadougou. <br>
  Due to French colonialism, the country's official language of government and business is French. <br>
  However, only 15% of the population actually speaks French on a regular basis. <br>
  There are 59 native languages spoken in Burkina, with the most common language, Moore, spoken by roughly 50% of Burkinabé. <br>

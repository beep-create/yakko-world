French Guiana is an overseas department/region and single territorial collectivity of France on the northern Atlantic coast of South America in the Guianas. <br>
It borders Brazil to the east and south and Suriname to the west.<br>
With a land area of 83,534 km2 (32,253 sq mi), French Guiana is the second-largest region of France and the largest outermost region within the European Union.<br> 
It has a very low population density, with only 3.5 inhabitants per square kilometre (9.1/sq mi). <br>
Half of its 294,071 inhabitants in 2021 lived in the metropolitan area of Cayenne, its capital. <br>
98.9% of the land territory of French Guiana is covered by forests, a large part of which is primeval rainforest. <br>
The Guiana Amazonian Park, which is the largest national park in the European Union, covers 41% of French Guiana's territory.<br>
